About Argentina

Argentina, officially known as the Argentine Republic, is the second-largest country in South America. It is bordered by Chile to the west, Bolivia and Paraguay to the north, Brazil and Uruguay to the northeast, and the Atlantic Ocean to the east. The country is known for its diverse landscapes, which range from the snowy Andes Mountains and the famous Patagonia region to vast grasslands called the Pampas. Argentina has a rich cultural heritage influenced by European immigration, especially from Spain and Italy. The country is well-known for tango music and dance, passionate soccer culture, and traditional foods like empanadas, milanesas, and the famous Argentine asado. The capital city, Buenos Aires, is one of the most vibrant cultural centers in Latin America, offering a mix of modern life and historic architecture.

My Life in Argentina

I served my mission in Buenos Aires West, also known as MISBAO. I really appreciated my time in Argentina and I came to love the people there. They treated me with so much kindness that I considered them like family, and the atmosphere I shared with them felt truly warm and close. The food they gave us was amazing, and spending time with the families and members was something I always enjoyed. I also loved drinking mate; even though we couldn’t share the same cup with others, it was interesting to see how they drank it no matter the weather—even in extreme heat or during the freezing winter! My mission helped me grow a lot as a person. I learned to socialize, to talk with people as if they were part of my own family, and I became more confident because before that I was very shy. I gained many life lessons during those two years, things I didn’t realize I needed to learn until I was there. To this day, I miss the people I met. They became like family to me.
